THE ANNUAL ASIAN BUSINESS LEADERS AWARDS

LOCALLY AWARDED. GLOBALLY RECOGNIZED.

Created by global business authority CNBC, the Asian Business Leaders Awards ABLA) have become one of the world’s most well known and most coveted awards. They are presented each year at a very fashionable closed-door gala dinner which is attended by some of the world’s most recognizable and most successful business people. The ABLA event is televised globally through the CNBC network and gives participants an instant introduction and reference all over the world. This year, with the long awaited introduction of the “I Am Future Unicorn” Award, small start up and emerging companies can finally participate in this important business summit.

15 years of growth

The ABLA event was created over 15 years ago and has gone from strength to strength. It is now one of the pre-eminent business awards and is recognized, by successful business people, all over the world.

78 Awardees

In all the world, only 78 people have ever received an Asian Business Leaders Award. It is a very exclusive club indeed and awardees are household names not only in Asia, but all over the globe for their vision, their invocative buses practices and their creative strategies.

1st Start up award.

This new “I Am Future Unicorn” Award, for the first time, lets the founders of small start ups mingle with the Big Boys of Business. The leaders of the emerging company that wins this award will be on equal footing with the true titans of business, and will have a seat at the table when new strategies, new markets and new opportunities are discussed.

6 Years Ahead

In awarding this honour to one promising start-up, the judges will be showing their belief that, within 6 years, the winning company will achieve true Unicorn status and have a market valuation/turnover of US$1 billion. So it is not only a prestigious award, but also a very high expectation that the winning start up leader will have to live up to.

HOW CAN I NOMINATE?

If you know of any fast-growing start-up that positively impacts Asia, has been in business for less than 7 years, and believe the company can achieve Unicorn status in the next 6 years, you should take advantage of this once in a lifetime opportunity. There are two easy ways to make a nomination for the 1st I Am Future Unicorn Award:

Personal nomination

Nominate a company as a potential Future Unicorn. Check the leaderboard regularly to see if the company you are rooting for is in the top 6 finalists and whether it eventually becomes the winner. (If it does, you could be rewarded by getting the chance to win further prizes)

The more nominations your company receives, the better chance you have of making a favourable impression on the judges. Personal voters get to spin the wheel of fortune on the website for instant prizes. Just ABLA’s way to thank you for taking the time to vote.

Corporate nomination

Companies can nominate themselves or other companies who they believe have the potential to become the Future Unicorn. Even employees of a company can nominate their own firm. It’s a great opportunity to boost company morale and promote team spirit. Which is always a good business practice.

Who is eligible to be nominated? Any start up or emerging company which has been trading in Asia for 7 years or less as of 1 August, 2016. (See Nomination Form, below, for full details).

Judging criteria

The judging panel will be comprised of people beyond reproach - esteemed representatives from The Singapore Institute of Management, as well as internationally respected management strategists, corporate personalities, renowned academics and experienced executives from Six Capital.

Judging will be utterly transparent and rigorous based on the following 8 categories:

UNICORN POSSIBILITES

Does the company and its leader have what it takes to achieve US$1 Billion Unicorn status within the next 6 years if it continues as it has been?

INNOVATION

Does the company and its leader exhibit innovation in terms of doing things in ways never done before?

CREATIVITY

Does the company and its leader show examples of solving problems and expanding markets in elegant, surprising ways?

BRANDING

Does the company and its leader made sufficient effort to separate itself from the pack and become recognized as a start up to watch?

SOCIAL CAPITAL

Has the company or its leader built up a bank account of charitable work, social enhancement and educational opportunities in the community?

POTENTIAL

Does the company or its leader have the management chops and experience to shepherd the firm to UNICORN status and keep it there?

CORPORATE SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY

Does the company or its leader conduct business in such a manner as to add to rather than subtract from the community and the world?

PUBLIC POLICY

Does the company or its leader have in place corporate policies which benefit the public in all aspects of its business activities?

As has been the case over the last 15 years with ABLA, judging for the I Am Future Unicorn Award will be very tough, but totally objective as well as utterly transparent.

Steps to being recognized as the Future Unicorn

Phase One Nomination 16 September, 2016

Both companies and individuals can nominate a start up company or start up leader which they feel exhibits the unique innovation, vision and ability to disrupt the status quo to become The Future Unicorn.

There can only be one.

Phase One Voting 1 October, 2016

After all qualified nominations have been assessed by the judging panel, six companies, who most embody the traits which a Unicorn company requires will be shortlisted and announced to the public.

Face-to-face interviews with the top executives of each of the Shortlist Six companies will be conducted to determine the winner.

Phase One Winner 4 November 2016

The very first I Am Future Unicorn Award winner will be selected by the judges and announced to the business community and to the public.

The inaugural I AM FUTURE UNICORN Award will be presented at the 15th anniversary ABLA dinner to be held in Jakarta Indonesia on November 23rd, 2016.

Good luck to all the nominees.
