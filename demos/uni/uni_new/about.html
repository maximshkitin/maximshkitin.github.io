I am future - Unicorn Award

The 15th ABLA will feature the inaugural SIXCAP Unicorn Award, an award that redefines the definition of a Unicorn. This award is to identify companies (both tech startups or established companies that are not quite there yet) that have the potential to reach either 1) 1 Billion Valuation or 1 Billion turnover, within the next 6 years (sooner is a bonus. This levels the playing field for both established companies and innovative startups to compete on the same platform.

The core theme of this award is #iamfuture. Conventional thinking would suggest that the "I" is the company, and how the company will be the future. However, we believe that the future is in harnessing the power of the crowd.

Unicorns of this day and age would not be able to get to where they are if it were not for the crowd behind them; therefore, the crowd is the future - and the companies that can successfully harness the power of the crowd - is bound to succeed. In the true spirit of the unicorn award, this award will tap into the crowd, sourcing the views of the crowd as to which companies will be the next Unicorn. Contrary to the convention of evaluating solely based on past accomplishments, the SIXCAP Unicorn Award is designed to be forward looking. The judging criteria will be based on a quantitative organization data analyses, crowd endorsement, and evaluation by an independent panel of management strategists, academics, and reputable industry practitioners.

A Unicorn - a phrase invented by Venture Capitalist Aileen Lee - refers to a start-up company, which attains a valuation of US$ 1 billion within a few years. Finding such a company, according to Lee “is as rare as finding a unicorn”.

To win big - think small

— Patrick Teng, Six Capital

Six Capital, a Singapore - based fintech company - will be the official sponsor of the 15th annual ABLA event as well as helping to judge for the I am Future Unicorn Award. This is especially apt as Six Capital, a data analytics specialist, has been a major player all over the world in arranging ingenious financing solutions and contrarian thinking for start ups and for well established companies. In this world of big picture globalization, Six Capital founder Patrick Teng, founder and CEO of has said “To win big - think small”. He puts that outlier philosophy to work every day in companies of all sizes to help reduce risk and fuel controlled expansion.

About CNBC ABLA

CNBC pioneered the annual Asia Business Leaders Awards (ABLA) in 2001 to salute and recognize remarkable business leaders, who through strength, innovation and foresight induce positive changes in an evolving corporate Asia.

Established in 2001, the CNBC ABLA is an annual award ceremony that recognizes the region’s best and inspirational business leaders who develop innovative strategies and best practices that help to shape the Asian economy and lead the region's growth, inducing positive changes in an evolving corporate Asia.

Last year, the 14th ABLA ceremony was held at the Siam Kempinski Hotel in Bangkok, Thailand. In recognition of astute business foresight and acumen, vision, innovation, talent management and corporate social responsibility. The recipients of the 14th CNBC ABLA award were Fast Retailing Co., Ltd. (Japan), Hitachi (Japan), IHH Healthcare (Malaysia), Malayan Banking (Malaysia), JG Summit Holdings (the Philippines) and Krung Thai Bank (Thailand).
